Oriental Beech

Fagus orientalis

Whole Plant View

Key Identification

Smooth, silvery-grey bark. Leaves are ovate-elliptic with few, blunt teeth, turning golden yellow in autumn.

Ecological Details

A large, deciduous tree dominating the Hyrcanian Forest canopy. It is a relict species from the Tertiary period.

Leaf Detail

Ovate-elliptic shape with wavy, non-serrated margins.

Trunk/Stem Detail

Characteristically smooth, thin, silvery-grey bark.

Persian Ironwood

Parrotia persica

Whole Plant View

Key Identification

Flaky bark showing shades of pink, green, and grey. Brilliant autumn colors (red, yellow, purple). Unique, bristly red flowers appear before the leaves.

Ecological Details

A monoecious, deciduous tree endemic to the Hyrcanian region. It is known for its beautiful, peeling bark and multi-stemmed structure.

Leaf Detail

Leaves show brilliant seasonal colors, from deep red to yellow and purple.

Trunk/Stem Detail

Mature bark peels to reveal stunning patches of different colors.

Caucasian Wingnut

Pterocarya fraxinifolia

Whole Plant View

Key Identification

Large, ash-like pinnate leaves up to 60 cm long. Fruits are long, hanging racemes (strings) of small, winged nuts.

Ecological Details

A fast-growing, large deciduous tree often found near water bodies and in lowland areas of the forest. It is notable for its massive size and dramatic appearance.

Leaf Detail

Massive compound leaves consisting of 7-25 leaflets.

Fruit/Seed Detail

Long, hanging strands of small, light-green, winged nuts.
