The Hyrcanian Forests: A Living Treasure

Nestled along the southern coast of the Caspian Sea, the Hyrcanian Forests form a unique ecological region that has survived for millions of years. These ancient forests are a UNESCO World Heritage site, recognized for their outstanding universal value and remarkable biodiversity.

Spanning from Azerbaijan to Iran, these lush woodlands are home to over 3,200 species of vascular plants, with many found nowhere else on Earth. The forests have persisted through multiple ice ages, creating a living laboratory of evolutionary history.

Today, the Hyrcanian Forests face threats from deforestation, climate change, and urban expansion. Our mission is to document, preserve, and raise awareness about this irreplaceable natural heritage before it's too late.

Featured Hyrcanian Species

Oriental Beech (Fagus orientalis)

A dominant tree species in Hyrcanian forests with distinctive smooth gray bark and oval leaves. These majestic trees can live for over 250 years.

View Details

Hyrcanian Boxwood (Buxus hyrcana)

An evergreen shrub with small, glossy leaves, important for soil conservation in the region. Unfortunately threatened by fungal diseases.

View Details

Persian Ironwood (Parrotia persica)

Known for its flaking bark and spectacular autumn colors, endemic to the Hyrcanian region. Its hard wood is resistant to decay.
